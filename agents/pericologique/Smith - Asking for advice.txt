[goes to meet someone in the park]

Smith's car ran hot. The things under the engine were getting old or some shit. The paint, a lot of the paint, had begun chipping off. On top of all this, it was never a good car to begin with. So Smith parked the beast a half mile away from the little spot on Mulholland Drive where teens went to drink and make-out at night, and where falcons sometimes swooped around and killed small animals.
	Smith wished he didn't dress up all businessy but he was meeting his old professor, and one of the few people in town he could take seriously.


"So what is it?"

"I negotiated a contract --" 

The professor interrupted, "With who?"

"This guy. It doesn't matter"

"Well, no shit some guy. Tell me who."

"Roosevelt Lowry with --"

"I know who he's with. Son of a bitch is a fucking shark."

"Well the deal fell through."

"Sounds like you're fucked, kiddo."

"Well, they don't know that it's fallen through yet."

"What does that even mean?"

"The contract we drew up was contingent on a piece of talent. The talent disappeared."

"Talent is always flakey. Why did you think that was a good idea in the first place?"

"It's my best friend. We landed a deal for all of us, but he was the lynch-pin that held it all together. We brought him to LA and cleaned him up. Then- poof! He's gone one night and the only person who might have seen him leave got locked up in the looney bin."

"Why do you need the guy? Last I heard from you, you were running a heavy show."

"Without him around to sign off, we can't use anything he touched."

"If he never shows up again, what does it matter?"

"I see your point."

"Of course you do." 

He paused and continued.

"Look. They don't know your boy's gone. If he shows up, sure, he could stop the show so to speak. But my money's on him never showing up again, and your crazy pal probably knows something you don't. Just go ahead with the contract like the guy never existed. You kept him in the back so you could stay in control of it all, right?"

"Yeah."

"Yeah, because you're a good producer. So produce. Conjure. Just stop fucking around waiting for permission. No one, not in this town, not on this goddamned planet, is going to give that to you."

"So just.."

"Fuck it, go back in and ask for more. Consider your losses. You lost a liability."

"Well, he was just there to bless it off."

"Fuck blessings."

"Fuck blessings."

"Sounds like you're good to go."

Professor Bird Man turned and walked back through the trees.
