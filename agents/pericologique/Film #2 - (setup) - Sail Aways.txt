The frothing sea waited. So did Kid Dracula and his entourage. They needed an answer out of Lester. He looked down at his feet, then at the wood beneath them, and searched for his shadow somewhere between the two, but could not find it. The sun tickled him but within hours it would begin torturing him.

	Atanase broke the silence, "Answer Căde."
	Lester replied, "What was it you wanted me to do? I forgot."
	Kid Dracula's eyes glowed with impatient rage, but he answered anyways.
	"I need you to make a film for me. Or we throw you off of this pier and you will drown."
	"Fair enough. What kind of film?" 
	"You decide. If it's good, we keep you around, if it's bad, we don't. It's that simple."
	Lester understood. 
	"Take this,"  Kid Dracula said, handing Lester the ratty script that Pavel had written. 
	"Your friend wrote it. It's horrible. Fix it and maybe we won't return to search for him."
	As Lester glossed over the first page, he recognized it was one of his own stories from long ago. Pavel was always just that very sort of scumbag. Something inside of Lester instilled in him the conviction that he should not share the revelation about Pavel.  

	"So I'm going to do a sequel to this."
	The entourage scanned Lester up and down as if his sudden cooperation shocked them. Lester could tell that there was a certain hierarchy going on. Atanase, the one who had so far addressed him with his gruff voice, was medium in build. The larger one, Refren, never spoke on his own, only took commands. Strofa resembled the remnants of an abortion and had a tiny voice to match. He seemed to avoid Kid Dracula and only interacted with Atanase. Lester wondered where their names came from. They didn't sound very name-like to him. Their accents confused him. He guessed that Kid Dracula was about his age so it further confused him as to why a bunch of grown men would hang around a pasty rich kid, unless they were his body guards.
	The back of Refren's hand met the side of Lester's face.
	"Answer him," Strofa demanded.
	"What was the question?" Lester asked. 
	"How soon can you come up with a five minute film?" Kid Dracula demanded.
	"Uh, ten days." [#]
	"That will be acceptable. You three will wait here in the beach house. Give him everything he needs but keep the leash tight."
	"Can we act again in this one, Căde?"
	"If he asks you to do so."
	Lester didn't want these rats shitting all over his work. So he tried to wiggle out of it.
	"I will need female actors for this."
	Kid Dracula and his entourage burst into laughter inexplicably. The entourage stayed in place, surrounding Lester as Kid Dracula walked back to his car. Kid Dracula looked back and said, "I'll send the twins over tomorrow. He'll have to make due with them."
	"Lucky you," Atanase remarked.
	Lester did not understand what was going on at all. But at least the beach house looked comfortable.



---- The next day, Lester got to work.
