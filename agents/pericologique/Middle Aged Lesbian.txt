A middle-aged person with a butch haircut and librarian glasses led the pack of audience members out of the cinema. Tears dripped down one cheek like wax on a fetish model's arse, its glimmer catching Fred's attention.
	Fred asked his fellow movie-goer, "How did you find it?" it being the movie they, as a crowd, had just seen. The other audience members didn't know what Fred did; that is, Fred knew who directed the cinematic piece that had been presented tonight, and he also knew that the audience as well as those operating the venue did not. "Butch" replied that it was the most beautiful movie ever to grace the silver screen. This made Fred's abdomen fill with the bliss of vindication and also happened to make his fists burn with anger.
	On the marquee in bright bold letters was someone else's name -- [NAME]. Although [NAME] received directorial credit and the applause of the audience, Fred saw through the counterfeit. Lester Lane, one of Fred's closest friends, had really directed the movie. For the last several months, Lester had been nowhere else but on the figurative back of a milk carton. Fred vomited on another audience member, one dressed so as to vaguely, but likely unintentionally, resemble Pee Wee Herman.
	"Sorry mate!" Fred said as he ducked back into the crowd, going back into the theatre foyer, past the stocky usher who looks as if he moonlights as a bouncer, through the darkness of the still-emptying, projector-still-running auditorium, all the way back to the rear exit. Outside, the cold crisp air and a homeless man with matted hair were all that awaited him. Fred threw up in the nearest garbage bin and walked to his car. On his drive home, his mind cycled through the movie as he tried his best to recreate what he had just seen.
