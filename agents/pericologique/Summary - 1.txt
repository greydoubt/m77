A group of rogue filmmakers (director, producer, two screenwriters, sound and photography, etc...) made a decent name for themselves in Hollywood.However, their secret was they were taking the notebooks of their talented but unmotivated mutual friend, and using that as a source for things. Their careers are hurting so they hatch a plot to kidnap him and bring him to LA to work on the movies and hopefully bring originality to the industry and jumpstart their careers.Once they orchestrate this, they reach another snag in their ambitions. One of the screenwriters, who moonlights as a script doctor for film students at USC, catering to wealthy but unskilled film department kids who need content, made a bad deal with a mob boss' kid.The mob boss' kid sends his lackeys to get back at the screenwriter, but they end up kidnapping the guy the filmmakers had kidnapped.After this, they take him out of the country and force him to make movies for the mob.For a few months, the filmmakers give up on ever getting their friend back but the mob movies begin receiving a lot of attention and critical acclaim on the independent movie circuit.There are a bunch of clues through-out them that one of them picks up on, and with that they start to track him down; while another member of the film group goes behind everyone's back and sells the rights to those films to a movie studio, claiming he can get the guy back.
